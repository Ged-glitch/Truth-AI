The problem

Generation is cheap. Verification is not.

Language models produce fluent text faster than anyone can check it against a reliable source of truth. Without a deterministic gate between generation and persistence, unverified claims become institutional memory.

01

AI content is increasing

Synthetic text now outpaces the human capacity to review it, and most of it is never checked against a source.

02

Claims are hard to verify

There is rarely a structured path from an assertion to the evidence that supports or contradicts it.

03

Evidence trails fragment

Sources scatter across tools and tabs, unhashed and unsnapshotted, so findings cannot be reproduced.

04

Audit trails are required

Compliance, legal and research teams need replayable decisions, not screenshots of a chat window.

The pipeline

From raw claim to auditable decision

Five deterministic stages. Every input is hashed; every output is replayable.

1

Submit claim

Atomic, decontextualised proposition with a claim type.

2

Ground in sources

Trusted online sources, references and files are snapshotted, frozen and hashed.

3

Extract entities

Subject–relation–object triples normalise the claim graph.

4

Run verification

The kernel checks each claim against your sources, references and the ledger.

5

Decision bundle

A signed, hashed record that replays the verdict exactly.

Core capabilities

Everything is typed, hashed and replayable

A deterministic kernel surrounded by the workflow tooling teams actually need.

Claim modelling

Atomic subject–relation–object triples with typed claim kinds and criticality tags.

Source & evidence packs

Trusted online sources, references, files and tool outputs snapshotted and bundled per claim.

Canonical hashing

RFC 8785 JSON and SHA‑256 content addressing for byte-equal replay.

Rule‑based verification

Versioned rule packs and pure predicates over a typed claim graph.

Decision bundles

Self-contained records of the graph, evidence, policy and verdict.

Audit ledger

Append-only, bitemporal, Merkle-chained store of accepted claims.

Human review workflow

Undecided claims route to reviewers with full context and provenance.

Project storage

Supabase-backed projects, claims, evidence and history per workspace.

Determinism & integrity

Measurement is deterministic. Generation is not.

The kernel makes no network calls, reads no clock and uses no randomness. Given the same inputs it returns byte-identical outputs — enforced by a 30-run replay check in CI.

SHA‑256 canonical hashes for every claim, evidence snapshot and bundle
Frozen JSON schemas — version-bumped, never silently changed
Reproducible outputs verified by byte-equal replay across runs
Testable rule packs with positive, negative and mutation coverage
Exportable audit trail as a Git-friendly, hash-chained event log
